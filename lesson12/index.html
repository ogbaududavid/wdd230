Discover Carlsbad

Encinitas

Another formerly sleepy beach town that screams 'grab your long board', Encinitas is now a top dining destination with celebrity chef Brian Malarkey's Herb & Sea and Eurobistro-wine bar Valentina. Another noteworthy addition to the area is Alila Marea, a luxury resort that recently opened its ocean front rooms and suites along with Vaga by acclaimed local Chef Claudette Zepeda and her Mexican-Asian signature dishes. The beauty of the property is its location—perched on a secluded coastal bluff with outdoor seating areas and firepits, you'll feel like you're on your own private beach. When you're not soaking in the view, stroll into town for the farmer's market or other coffee shops and cafes.

Ventura

Sandwiched in between Carpinteria and Oxnard, Ventura Harbor Village is a working harbor, fishing marina and quaint area with shops, galleries, restaurants and waterfront activities. Expect a carousel, stand-up paddle-board and jet skiing with sea lions, a beach cove and surfers, plus it's dog friendly. This spot is also where you will find the Channel Islands National Park Visitor Center, aka “The American Galapagos,” with a viewing tower where you can spot up to 445 species of birds.

Long Beach

It's no small town, and it's located just 20 miles from downtown L.A., but the allure of Long Beach escapes most Angelenos anyway. (Excepting Lana Del Rey, who highlights the place in songs and videos featuring her love of Cali.) So listen up: There's a case to be made for Long Beach as your new beachy getaway, with its ethnic diversity, great food and walkable downtown area. Stay at the jazzy yet affordable Hotel Maya and be sure to visit the docked QE II or the reopened Aquarium of the Pacific. In the well-healed Belmont Shores section along Pacific Coast Highway, L.A. nightlife guru Brent Bolthouse and Michelin-starred chef Michael Mina have opened a stunning Bungalow Kitchen outpost designed by Studio Collective.
